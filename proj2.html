For the Divine in you!


Baroque speaks about extravagance. I wanted to take inspiration from the Roman Catholic Church. How the pope being the head of the religion is on the spot light and is surrounded by divine light. I wondered if he became the celebrity endorsement for a brand, would people buy extras? Here is an attempt, where the gelato ice cream is adorned with a bedazzled pope’s crown to show how it is indeed the head of the ice cream industry not only that - it is pope recommended. In the divine light, among the dark shadows, the ice cream melts in dramatic action to only show that, “have the gelato and find the divine in you!”







One Sip Closer to Nature


The true essence of romanticism. Suramya is exactly how you feel when you take a sip of this wine as you drink it. One sip closer to nature! I wanted to capture the sheer magic of mother nature. The way she those vines drop to give us, flavourful and juicy grapes which are then crushed by the most natural way using two bare hands to reveal the juiced fruits. Suramya is exactly how you feel when you take a sip of this wine as you drink it. One sip closer to nature! After I chose romanticism as my art movement, I decided to start with looking at the picture, the paintings, to go over the poetry of Wordsworth and feel it once again. I moved on to start reading about the romantic art movement. It beinga silent revolt towards the industrial revolution. Going forward I read about the importance and significance of wine throughout time to find so many interesting facts about its bottling and drinking habits. I started with initial sketches that resulted in a lot of digital painting and mockups to end it.











For the human in you


Why Coffee?-A drink that saw it all. Post Impressionism was a movement that was a peak inside the mind of every artist. A window into their thinking. But what might have made the artists not paint the surroundings but just take inspiration? Artists, people, authors started sharing their ideas over a coffee in many parts of France as the cafe culture began. The sharing of ideas, the blending of various classes and groups and women spending time in these places made them flourish in the most beautiful way. And it was all over some good old coffee. Coffee was indeed a drink that paved the way to the most basic thing that made us all human. The spark of the conversation over some coffee made some normal people into greatness.
